Mashed Potatoes Recipe

Description

Mashed potatoes are a great side dish to many other meals that can be enjoyed any time of the year! It is a staple dish for the Thanksgiving holiday! It is a simple recipe typically consisting of mashing together boiled or steamed potatoes and adding a few ingredients such as butter and salt! It can be made far more complex but for the purposes of this recipe, it will be kept simple.

Ingredients

  • 2 Pounds Baking Potatoes, Peeled And Quartered
  • 3 Cloves Garlic, Peeled, Or To Taste (Optional Ingredient)
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 2 Tablespoons Butter
  • Salt And Ground Black Pepper To Taste

Steps

  1. Add water to a large pot and add salt to the water. Add potatoes and garlic once the water has started boiling. Lower the heat to medium at this point and simmer for around 15-20 minutes, by which the potatoes should be tender.
  2. Meanwhile, heat milk and butter into a small saucepan on low heat until the butter is melted.
  3. Drain the water in the potatoes pot and slowly add the warm milk mixture. Blend it with an electric mixer or potato masher until the mixture is smooth and creamy. At this point, season with salt and pepper and enjoy!

Tip: Skim, Low-Fat, Whole Milk, Or Half-And-Half May Be Used To Enrich The Flavor!
